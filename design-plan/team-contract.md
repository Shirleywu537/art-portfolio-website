# Team Contract

Working together as a team is critical for your success on this project. Conflicts are normal and to be expected. It's best to develop a plan for handling conflicts before they arise.

**This contract is a _living_ document.** If it's not working, your team should revise it as often as necessary.

## Contract

Your team contract should probably include these elements. Feel free to remove or add other terms, too.

1. To what goals do we all commit?

    - putting forth our best effort and implementing best practices in our work
    - ensuring our client is satisfied with our work
    - communicating properly and in a timely manner with our teammates
    - fostering a safe and efficient atmosphere during all meetings

2. What are our "ground rules"? (Don't make an exhaustive list—stick with just a few.)

    **Tip:** Interrupting another person can make them feel like their option isn't valued. Consider a rule about interrupting.

    - respect the one mic policy, don't interrupt when others have the floor
    - keep in contact, don't ignore communication
    - have every part done by the agreed-upon deadline
    - clearly communicate your ideas, don't assume everyone will understand right off the bat
    - ask questions if necessary! don't be afraid to ask for help
    - communicate when working on the project so there is no confusion or merge errors

3. By what means and how frequently will we meet or communicate?

    - means: text groupchat
    - meet: once a week in person for an hour, schedule Zoom meeting if necessary

4. How will we communicate and work with our client?

    - Via email and have virtual meetings.
    - Have open communication via email in case either party has questions.

5. What roles will we create? Include a description of exactly what each role is responsible for. (**No "coder" or "designer" roles are permitted;** everyone must design and code.)

    **Tip:** It's often helpful to think of **responsibilities** rather than roles here.

    - notetaker: jot down notes during meetings
    - final submitter: last glance and fill out the submission form
    - office hour attendee: ask questions and attend office hours
    - primary client communicator: be the client's 'go to' and express the clients' needs

6. How will we hold ourselves and one another accountable? What (exactly) will we say to a team member who appears not to be contributing equally? To a team member who fails to deliver what was promised?

    - Fairly splitting out different tasks
    - Be clear about when tasks need to be done
    - Bring it up in our weekly meeting if a team member appears not to be contributing equally
    - Contact a member of the teaching team if the issues persist

7. What do we do when we disagree? What approach to conflict to we all agree to uphold?

    - First, hear out everyone's perspective.
    - Second, try to compromise in a way that is fair to as many opinions as possible.
    - If compromise isn't possible, do majority-rules voting.

8. What are our procedures for re-visiting this contract?

    - When a team member notices that we have strayed too far from the best practices outlined in this contract, they will kindly remind everyone to reread the contract.
    - From there, we will discuss how to stay on track in the future.

9. What else do we want to include in this contract? Maybe you team needs a _fun_ clause...

    **Tip:** Not everything in life is about work. Sometimes it is important to celebrate too. Consider grabbing a coffee or ice cream at the completion of your project to celebrate!

    - After we submit the final project, we will get food together!

10. If someone has an emergency/injury/etc. that prevents them from contributing, what should they do?

    - They should communicate with the group with at least 24 hours notice, and also report their circumstances to course administration.

## Using your Contract

You are expected to follow your team contract. **If you are having issues (e.g. conflicts) in your team, the first thing you should do is follow the contract.** If the contract isn't working, then your team should revise it.

If you cannot resolve your conflict using the contract, please email the instructors at <info1300@cornell.edu>. Please be advised, that the first thing the instructor will ask you is, "did you try to resolve your issue with the contract?" The next question the instructor will ask you is, "what change did you make to your contract to resolve the issue?" You must attempt to resolve your conflicts on your own first before an instructor will intervene.
